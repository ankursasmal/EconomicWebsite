Inflation And It Effects
Inflation Inflation is a gradual loss of purchasing power, reflected in a broad rise in prices for goods and services over time

Types of inflation
Cost-Push Inflation
1.Increase in price of inputs 2. Hoarding and Speculation of commodities 3. Defective Supply chain 4. Increase in indirect taxes 5. Depreciation of Currency 6. Crude oil price fluctuation 7. Defective food supply chain 8. Low growth of Agricultural sector 9. Food Inflation 10. Interest rates increased by RBI
Cost Push inflation
1.Government spending or Deficit financing by the government – When the government spends more freely, prices go up. 2.Due to fiscal stimulus. 3.Increased borrowing. 4. Depreciation of rupee. 5.Low unemployment rate.
Built-in Inflation
1. As demand-pull inflation and cost-push inflation occur, employees may start asking employers for a raise. If employers don’t keep their wages competitive, they could end up with a labor shortage. 2. If a business raises workers’ wages or salaries and tries to maintain profit margins by raising prices, that’s built-in inflation. 3. Now, if you learn about your favorite coffeehouse raising prices due to the climbing cost of coffee bean.
Effects of inflation
1.Economic growth. 2. More consumer spending 3. Higher wages. 4. Assets increase in value 5. Easier loan repayment. 6. Higher prices for basic goods. 7. Savings depreciate. 8. Lower global demand. 9. Economic uncertainty. 10. Disproportionately affects lower-income people
